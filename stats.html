About De-Suup?

"De" originates from "Dekyid" which means peace. "Suung" refers to guarding. Therefore, Desuung means "Guardians of Peace". Launched on February 14, 2011 by the fifth King of Bhutan, Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck, the De-suung programme aims to encourage citizens to take up a role in nation-building.

TARA(Dolma)

  • Similarly, there are innumerable manifestations of Tara (Dolma), one of the most beloved deities. Tara is also known as "Mother of liberation" and "goddess of compassion". There are said to be twenty-one taras and the two most popular ones are White Tara and Green Tara. It is said that these goddesses of compassion wept tears for the beings of samsara. One can differentiate between the two Taras by looking at the colour of the lotus held on their hand. Many believe that if the male pays homage to Tara and chants prayers, good things will befall the believers.

Ema Datshe

Bhutan is well know for Bhutanese cuisine is its spiciness. it is our national dish. it is a simple stew made of a variety of chilies and special cheese . people of Bhutan love to eat spicy curry special ema datshi which is very popular to tourist. chilly are an essential part of every dish and are considered so important that most of the Bhutanese would not take meal without spicy. It is one the popular cuisine that make every visitor to drag to visit Bhutan for this only to taste the curry.

Kewa Datshe

Basically kewa datshi means curry made from potato and cheese. Kewa datshi is least spicy compared to ama datshi and most of the tourist loves this dish. It is one of the most eaten dish in Bhutan,loved by both elder people and kids.

Lamd Bhuna

Bhunna in Urdu means "to be fried" so the Bhuna curry is another cooking style rather than a specific set of ingredients. So although considered by many as a type of curry dish, bhuna is actually a cooking technique whereby a mix of spices are fried in very hot oil in order to make a paste. The meat is then fried together with this paste mix giving a thick intense dish.

MoMo

Momo is a staple snack (if there can be one) in Bhutanese cuisines. Momos are 'dumplings' made from palm-sized wheat dough sheets stuffed with fillings of myriad choices like meat, cheese and or vegetable fillings. Momo has found its footing as the most loved and must-have snack in Bhutan though it's believed to have its origin in Chinese/Tibetan cuisines. There's little doubt why it shouldn't be on the list of the must-try-Bhutanese-dishes while in Bhutan
